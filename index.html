Abstract

This is a placeholder abstract. We will replace it with the final version before camera-ready.

Audition-based search and rescue with drones requires robust perception under strong, non-stationary ego-noise produced by rotors and airframe vibrations. We present DroneAudioNet, a noise-suppression framework that combines a lightweight neural enhancement model with a task-oriented training recipe to improve downstream acoustic event detection and localization. On a suite of challenging drone-recorded scenarios, our approach yields consistent gains in perceptual quality and detection performance compared to classical baselines and recent learning-based enhancers, while maintaining low latency suitable for onboard deployment.
